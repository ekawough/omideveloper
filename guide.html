Use case 1: Tell your car what to do (Tesla Connect)

If you drive a Tesla, the Omi turns it into a voice command device.

  • "Hey Omi, unlock my Tesla."
  • "Hey Omi, set climate to 70."
  • "Hey Omi, open the frunk."
  • "Hey Omi, where am I parked?"

The app is in the App Store, free to install. I built it. It is the one I use every day.

Why this matters: You stop touching your phone in the car. Hands stay on the wheel.

Use case 2: Stop writing showing notes (Showing Notes)

If you sell real estate, this is the use case.

You wear the Omi during a buyer showing. You walk through the house and talk like you normally would. Granite is dated, buyer loves the natural light, worried about the HOA dues, schedule a second visit Saturday.

By the time you sit down in your car, a buyer ready showing report is already in your buyer's inbox. Branded with your headshot.

What it is replacing: 2 hours of typing showing notes at 11 pm with a glass of wine.

Use case 3: Auto update your CRM (SalesSignal)

If you sell anything in person or on Zoom, this is the one.

You wear the Omi during the meeting. You walk to your car. By the time you sit down, your CRM has the meeting summary, the next step, the contact's pain points, and a draft follow up email ready to send.

Bring your own CRM. Works with HubSpot, Salesforce, GoHighLevel, Pipedrive, Close, Follow Up Boss, Compass, kvCORE, Insightly, Zoho, Copper, and anything with a public API. Connect once, the rest is automatic.

What it is replacing: 30 minutes of CRM hygiene per meeting. If you take 5 meetings a day, that is 12.5 hours per week.

Use case 4: Field service reports without typing (Field Report)

If you run an HVAC, plumbing, roofing, or solar crew, this is the one.

Tech wears the Omi. Walks the roof or the job. Talks like a normal field tech. South facing array, panel needs replacement, homeowner concerned about HOA approval.

Five minutes later, a structured site report lands in the dispatch system. PDF version goes to the owner.

What it is replacing: 2 hours of reports per tech per day. If you have 5 techs, that is 50 hours of overtime per week.

Use case 5: Daily Wrapped Pro (free, runs in the background)

This one is free. Every Sunday it produces a Spotify Wrapped style recap of your week.

  • Hours spoken
  • Top topics
  • Top people
  • Best quote of the week
  • One tap share to Instagram Story

It is the cleanest "what was my week actually about" view you can get without writing a journal entry.

Why I built it: it is free for everyone, and it makes the Omi worth wearing on slow days.
