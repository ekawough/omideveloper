// faq

Frequently asked.

Do I need to wear the Omi the whole day, or only at showings?
Only at showings. The Omi battery lasts 16 to 18 hours so you can wear it all day if you want, but Showing Notes only processes audio when you tag a showing in the app or when the property address is detected. No background processing of your private day.
Will my buyer know I am recording?
California is a two party consent state. The recommended workflow is the same as taking notes on a tablet: mention casually that you take voice notes during showings so you can send them a recap. Buyers expect a recap. Most buyers prefer a recap to a text the next day. The Omi has a one tap delete button on the device for any conversation you do not want stored.
What if the showing has a noisy AC or kids running around?
The Omi mic is optimized for conversational distance. It handles normal showing audio well: walking, talking, narrating to yourself or a buyer. Loud kitchens and outdoor crowds drop accuracy somewhat but the post processing fills in context. Worst case the PDF is 90 percent right and you edit one paragraph.
How does it know my buyer email and name?
First showing with a buyer, you tag them once in the app. After that the Showing Notes engine recognizes the property and auto routes the recap to the right buyer profile. You can also CC yourself or your TC, or push to Follow Up Boss / Compass / kvCORE if you have those connected.
Does it work with Plaud Note?
Yes, four of the six Omi apps I built run on Plaud through the xor connection. Showing Notes is one of them. Setup is 90 seconds via the Omi mobile app. The Plaud guide on this site walks you through it.
What if I cancel during the free trial?
No card required to start the trial. If you cancel, nothing was charged. If you keep going past day 7, the card you eventually add gets charged $29 per month. Cancel from the dashboard, no email loop.
Will this replace my CRM?
No. Showing Notes pushes recap PDFs and buyer profile updates into whatever CRM you already use. The CRM is your system of record. Showing Notes is the input layer.
