Refresh

Everything you need to flash, debug, and develop custom firmware for the Omi AI wearable.

What's Inside

SEGGER J-Link debugger: Industry-standard SWD/JTAG programmer for the nRF5340

J-Link to USB cable:  Connect the debugger to your development machine

2-pin jumper wires: For connecting to Omi's SWD debug pads

Omi Flash PCB: Custom breakout board that interfaces directly with the Omi hardware, providing easy access to the nRF5340's debug interface without disassembly

Built For

Embedded engineers building custom firmware for the nRF5340 dual-core SoC

Developers extending Omi's BLE 5.3 and Wi-Fi 6 (nRF7002) capabilities

Teams integrating Omi into custom workflows, enterprise deployments, or research applications

Anyone who wants to go beyond the stock firmware and make Omi do exactly what they need

Compatible With

Omi wearable (nRF5340-CLAA + nRF7002)

nRF Connect SDK (Zephyr RTOS)

SEGGER Embedded Studio , VS Code + nRF Extension , and other standard ARM toolchains

macOS, Windows, and Linux

Getting Started

Connect the J-Link to your computer via USB

Attach the Flash PCB to your Omi device

Wire the 2-pin SWD connection

Flash using nRF Connect SDK, J-Link Commander, or your preferred IDE

Full documentation and example firmware available at github.com/BasedHardware/omi 

Why This Kit?

Omi is fully open-source: hardware, firmware, and software. This kit gives you physical access to the debug interface so you can modify every layer of the stack. No locked bootloaders, no proprietary toolchains, no permission needed.
