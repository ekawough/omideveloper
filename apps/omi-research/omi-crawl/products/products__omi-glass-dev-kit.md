What are the technical specifications of Omi Glass? 

Target user / build: developer-focused 
 
DevKit ; requires manual assembly (3D-printed parts + wiring + firmware flashing)
 
MCU / main board: Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32 S3 Sense 
 
SoC family: ESP32-S3 
 
Camera: integrated camera on XIAO ESP32 S3 Sense board
 
Power (batteries): 

6 × 150 mAh LiPo batteries (main supply)

1 × 250 mAh LiPo battery (additional)

Mechanical / enclosure: 3D-printed mount/case (STL files in hardware folder)
 
Hinges: standard glasses hinges
 
Power switch: not included in the “current design” referenced in your text (design may vary by revision)
 
Firmware tooling: Arduino IDE (or arduino-cli ) - ESP32 board package via Espressif Arduino core
 
PSRAM setting: OPI PSRAM (per setup steps)
 
Software stack: Expo project; Node tooling ( npm or yarn )
 
Repo path: omi/OmiGlass within https://github.com/BasedHardware/omi 
 
Water-resistant : No

svg]:!gp-h-[var(--height-iconCollapseSize)] [&>svg]:!gp-w-full">

How does it work? 

It takes pictures every few seconds and sends them to omi AI app, pictures are used to give AI more context and help you with advices during the day
 
It can also record video and audio but you'll need to modify default firmware

svg]:!gp-h-[var(--height-iconCollapseSize)] [&>svg]:!gp-w-full">

How safe is my data?   

Your data is designed to stay under your control. 
 
Omi uses enterprise-grade safeguards: it's SOC 2 and HIPAA compliance, encrypts data in transit (TLS) and at rest (AES-256), and can run locally so you can avoid relying on any cloud (manual setting). 
 
You can also export everything easily whenever you want, and if you decide to leave, you can delete items or wipe your data entirely.
