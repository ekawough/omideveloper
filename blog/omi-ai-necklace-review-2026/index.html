What the Omi AI Necklace Actually Is

Omi is a small, lightweight AI wearable you wear around your neck. It has a microphone that passively captures ambient audio throughout your day. That audio gets transcribed in near real time and sent to an AI processing layer that can trigger apps, create memories, log conversations, and fire custom webhooks.

The hardware in 2026 is on its second major revision. The form factor is smaller than the original. The clip mechanism is more reliable. Battery life sits at about 24 hours of passive listening on a single charge, though real-world use with active recording is closer to 16 to 18 hours.

The device pairs to your phone over Bluetooth. There is an iOS app and an Android app. Both work. The Android app still lags slightly behind iOS on notification handling but the core recording pipeline is identical.

30-Day Wear Test: What I Actually Observed

Days 1 to 7: Calibration and Setup

The first week was calibration. Getting the device set up with my custom apps, wiring the webhooks, confirming Supabase writes were coming through reliably. For a non-developer, this period is simpler: download the app, pair the device, choose which built-in AI features you want active.

The out-of-box experience in 2026 is significantly better than early 2025. Battery life held at 16 to 18 hours for me, which covers a full waking day. I charged it overnight every night and never ran out mid-day.

Days 8 to 20: The Voice Control Habit Loop

By the second week, I had internalized the Tesla voice control to the point where it felt weird not to have it. The round-trip time from voice command to vehicle acknowledgment is about 3 to 4 seconds on a good LTE connection.

Life Logger became my primary note-taking system. I stopped manually opening note apps. By day 15, I had a searchable record of everything I had discussed, planned, or verbally brainstormed for two straight weeks.

Days 21 to 30: The Long Tail Test

The final stretch was about durability. Battery still hits 15 to 16 hours. No reliability issues. The one real friction point: the device does not handle very loud environments well. This is a known limitation of the hardware and the 2026 revision has not fully solved it.
